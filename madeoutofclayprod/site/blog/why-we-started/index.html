Our Story

Why We Started Made Out of Clay Productions

By Isaiah Hartwell · June 2026

Every children's book shelf tells a story about who we think matters. Walk through any library or bookstore and look at what's there — whose families are centered, whose differences are celebrated, whose kids get to be the heroes. For a long time, the answer was narrow. And children who didn't fit that narrow frame had to either make themselves smaller or quietly decide that stories weren't really for them.

That's the problem Made Out of Clay Productions was built to solve.

Three series, one promise

When we sat down to map out what this imprint would publish, we didn't start with a market opportunity. We started with the children we knew — kids who were neurodivergent, kids who came from complicated family structures, kids who needed a lion kingdom adventure that also quietly taught them something about faith and belonging. Three distinct series emerged from that starting point, each one aimed at a different slice of childhood that doesn't get enough attention.

At Sunny's Table is about a sloth family navigating the ordinary chaos of family life — waiting, listening, the particular patience that comes when you're seven and dinner isn't ready and the world feels like it's moving too slow. At the center of every story in this series is Sunny, the father, who always speaks last. Not because he doesn't have anything to say — but because he waits until he knows what matters. That's the model we want kids to absorb: that wisdom often comes after listening.

Just the Way You Are came from a real child. Clayton — the boy behind every book in this series — has ADHD and autism, and for years the books available to him either treated his differences as problems to solve or as inspiration fodder for neurotypical readers. We wanted something different: books that put Clayton's experience at the center, written in identity-first language, with a "Note to Grown-Ups" that gives caregivers actual tools instead of empty affirmations. Every book in this series has been through a sensitivity review. That's not optional here.

The Kingdom of Judea is the adventure series — lions, highlands, a boy named Caleb who is trying to understand what home means when home is complicated. There's faith woven through these stories, but lightly. The values are there in the way characters treat each other, in the choices Caleb makes when nobody's watching. It's the kind of series that asks big questions in small moments.

What "Made Out of Clay" means

The name is literal and not. Every one of these characters was shaped by hand — drafted, revised, argued over, re-drawn. But the name also points at something we believe: that children are made out of clay too. Still forming. Still becoming. The books we put in their hands while they're forming matter. We don't take that lightly.

Nine books are in the world now. More are coming. We hope they find the children who need them most.
